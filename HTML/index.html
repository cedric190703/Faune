What is fauna and flora?

Fauna :

All the plants that grow on land and in the seas make up the flora. There are large trees as well as small grasses, and even seaweed.

Botany is an exciting science, due to the infinite number and variety of plants that can be classified into families. Small plants are collected by drying them flat and classifying them in a herbarium, after consulting a special dictionary, also called a flora, which helps to recognize them.

By the feast of floralies, the Romans honored Flora, the goddess of flowers and plants. Nowadays, floralies have become great horticultural exhibitions that attract many admiring visitors.

Flora :

When we want to designate all the animals, from the smallest to the largest, that live in a country, we say: the fauna of that country.

Wildlife changes from country to country. It depends a lot on the flora and the conditions of adaptation to the environment. Indeed, only well-adapted animals survive. An insectivore, for example, can only thrive in a place where the insects it makes its food exist.

Faced with the local disappearance of insects, it can only survive by emigrating or gradually adapting to another form of food. Ecology studies the relationship of life between animals, plants and the natural environment.
