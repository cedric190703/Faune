Mammiferes :

This section tells all about the animals that most resemble us — that's what mammals are all about. Now let's see what we have in common with other mammals. They are warm-blooded animals; their heart has four chambers and they are filled with mammary glands. They give birth to already fully formed young (in comparison to other animals that lay eggs) and are covered with hair, in whole or in part, depending on the species.

The teeth of most mammals come in various shapes and sizes, with these being designed to chew various types of food. In humans, for example, the incisors are used to cut food and molars, grind it.

In addition, the brains of mammals are larger than those of other vertebrate animals of the same size, which gives them a greater ability to learn. That explains your ability to read this interesting fact! There is so much to learn about mammals, so take the time to go through this section.
