Fish, Amphibians and Reptiles :

Some vertebrates do not need feathers or hair on their body to keep warm. They are ectothermic, or "cold-blooded". This means that their internal temperature is identical to that of their environment, which is the case for fish, amphibians and reptiles.

Our country contains nearly a quarter of the Earth's freshwater reserves. We therefore have a certain responsibility to conserve the 230 species of fish living in our lakes, rivers and streams. Since any water flowing in Canada will eventually go to the ocean, pollutants entering lakes and streams far from the sea can still end up there.

Amphibians, represented by frogs and salamanders in Canada, also depend on freshwater ecosystems for their survival. Their eggs do not have a hard shell and should be left in water or in a very humid environment. Amphibians do not have scales and their skin should remain clammy.
